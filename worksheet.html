1.2 (b)

We know that the density function of a normal variable is:

\(X \sim N(\mu , \sigma^{2}) \longrightarrow f_X (x)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi \sigma^{2}}} e^{-\dfrac{(x-\mu)^{2}}{2\sigma^{2}}}\)

Likelihood for the i-th individual, using the above density and the given function for \(\mu\) , can be written as

\(L_i (\theta_{1} , \theta_{2}, \sigma^{2})=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi \sigma^{2}}} e^{-\dfrac{(y_i - \frac{\theta_{1}x_i}{\theta_{2}+x_i})^{2}}{2\sigma^{2}}}\).

Also, we know that the full likelihood is the product of the likelihood for individuals, having the mathematical relation as

\(L (\theta_{1} , \theta_{2}, \sigma^{2})= \prod_{i=1}^{n}{L_i (\theta_{1} , \theta_{2}, \sigma^{2})}\).

Thus, in this example, as we know that \(n=100\), we have:

\(L (\theta_{1} , \theta_{2}, \sigma^{2})=(2\pi \sigma^{2})^{-\dfrac{100}{2}} e^{-\dfrac{\sum_{i=1}^{100} (y_i - \frac{\theta_{1}x_i}{\theta_{2}+x_i})^{2}}{2\sigma^{2}}}\).

Taking logarithm from the obtained equation gives the equation for log-likelihood:

\(l (\theta_{1} , \theta_{2}, \sigma^{2})=-\dfrac{100}{2}log(2\pi \sigma^{2}) {-\dfrac{\sum_{i=1}^{100}(y_i-\frac{\theta_{1}x_i}{\theta_{2}+x_i})^{2}}{2\sigma^{2}}}\)

There are several problems if you try to apply linear regression for this kind of data.

The diagnostic plots are shown in the following:

#install.packages("ggfortify")
library(ggfortify)

The diagnostic plots show residuals in four different ways:

  1. Residuals vs Fitted. Used to check the linear relationship assumptions. A horizontal line, without distinct patterns is an indication for a linear relationship, what is good.

  2. Normal Q-Q. Used to examine whether the residuals are normally distributed. Its good if residuals points follow the straight dashed line.

  3. Scale-Location (or Spread-Location). Used to check the homogeneity of variance of the residuals (homoscedasticity). Horizontal line with equally spread points is a good indication of homoscedasticity. This is not the case in our example, where we have a heteroscedasticity problem.

  4. Residuals vs Leverage. Used to identify influential cases, that is extreme values that might influence the regression results when included or excluded from the analysis. This plot will be described further in the next sections.

In these plots, outliers are also marked with their observation number.

The usual way of extending a model is to include other variables in the model, if there is any, and also check for their two-way interaction. Here in the dataset, we also have information about the quarter of the years. So we include them into the model and check its two-way interaction with the other variate of interest, namely Dates.
